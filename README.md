# Maternal Mental Health and Its Impact on Infant Sleep

## Business Task
The central research question of this project is to determine what factor(s) influence the frequency of nighttime awakenings in infants during their first year of life. The analysis specifically explores the link between maternal well-being—including factors like maternal age, marital status, and postpartum depression—and infant sleep outcomes.

## Data Set
The study utilizes several continuous and categorical variables:
**Maternal Mental Health**: Measured via the Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS), Hospital Anxiety and Depression Scale (HADS), and City Birth Trauma Scale (CBTS).
**Infant Sleep Metrics**: Includes infant night awakenings and total sleep duration.
**Demographics**: Maternal age and marital status (Single, In a relationship, Separated/Divorced, or Other).
**Infant Context**: Infant age categories ranging from 3 to 12 months and the specific method used to put the infant to sleep (e.g., while being fed vs. alone in a crib).

## Tools
**Statistical Analysis**: Used to identify significant correlations and perform sensitivity analysis on variables.
**Data Visualization**: Documentation of interconnected family factors through visual charts.
**Collaborative Reporting**: Developed by the Unison Park project team.

---

## 📊 Key Findings & Analysis
The research highlights several critical insights regarding infant sleep patterns:
**Maternal Mental Health Correlation**: There is a significant correlation between increased maternal depressive/anxiety symptoms and frequent infant night waking.
**Bedtime Interventions**: Infant sleep continuity is highly sensitive to external factors, specifically the method of parental intervention used at bedtime.
**Non-Significant Variables**: The age of the mother and her marital status were not found to be statistically significant factors in this study.
